🤔 Wait, why use an "Intensity Proxy" instead of just temperature?

If you're wondering why we didn't just plot the raw temperature on the graph, here's the deal:

Think of a hurricane like a car. The Energy (Min Temp) is the raw horsepower, and the Structure (Cold Fraction) is the transmission. You can have a massive engine, but if the transmission is broken (like when wind shear blows the storm apart), the car isn't going anywhere.

We built the Intensity Proxy to act as a single "speedometer." It ensures the line on our chart only spikes when the storm has both the raw energy and the structural organization to actually use it.

The Math:

We calculated this by setting a baseline for deep convection and rewarding the storm for being both cold and symmetrical:

Iproxy = w1(210 - Tmin) + w2(fcold × 100)
  • 210 K: The baseline temperature where serious convection starts.
  • Tmin: The coldest cloud top we found in the frame.
  • fcold: How much of the storm's core is covered by those cold clouds.
  • w1, w2: Balancing weights to keep the graph readable.
